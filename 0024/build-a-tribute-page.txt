          <h2>Timeline of Ayrton Senna’s Life and Career</h2>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>1960</strong> – Born on March 21 in São Paulo, Brazil.</li>
                <li><strong>1973</strong> – Begins kart racing at age 13, quickly showing natural talent.</li>
                <li><strong>1981</strong> – Moves to England to compete in single-seater racing, winning the Formula
                    Ford 1600 Championship.</li>
                <li><strong>1983</strong> – Wins the British Formula 3 Championship, gaining attention from Formula 1
                    teams.</li>
                <li><strong>1984</strong> – Makes his Formula 1 debut with Toleman; impresses in the Monaco Grand Prix,
                    finishing second in the rain.</li>
                <li><strong>1985</strong> – Joins Lotus and achieves his first F1 victory at the Portuguese Grand Prix.
                </li>
                <li><strong>1988</strong> – Moves to McLaren; wins his first World Championship, beginning a legendary
                    rivalry with Alain Prost.</li>
                <li><strong>1990</strong> – Wins his second Formula 1 World Championship.</li>
                <li><strong>1991</strong> – Wins his third Formula 1 World Championship, celebrated with immense pride
                    in Brazil.</li>
                <li><strong>1994</strong> – Tragically passes away on May 1 during the San Marino Grand Prix at Imola,
                    Italy. His death profoundly impacts Formula 1 safety reforms.</li>
            </ul>

            <div class="quote">
