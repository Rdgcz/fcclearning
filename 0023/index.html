Dame Octavia Crumb

Patron Saint of Tea, Biscuits, and Delightfully Unnecessary Experiments

A Timeline of Octavia’s Boldly Pointless Brilliance

  • 1983 — Born in Dorset, England, with a teacup in hand. Doctors insist the cup was “BYO.”
  • 1990 — Establishes the Junior Society of Overly Specific Hobbies; first paper: On the Ethical Treatment of Spoon Collections.
  • 1998 — Discovers the Fifth Temperature of Tea (“Perilously Sippable”) and writes a 74-page etiquette addendum.
  • 2004 — At university, pioneers Controlled Biscuit Immersion (CBI), proving the biscuit’s natural enemy is hubris.
  • 2009 — Patents the Self-Stirring Saucer; it sells tens of units, mostly to her aunt Beatrice.
  • 2012 — Trains pigeons to deliver handwritten compliments; London morale jumps 3% (margin of error: ±3%).
  • 2015 — Launches the world’s quietest parade. It is so respectful that three participants nap throughout.
  • 2018 — Publishes The Physics of Crumbs, introducing the Crumble Constant (κ); bakers nod gravely.
  • 2020 — Invents Long-Distance High-Tea via synchronized kettles and dramatic nodding on video calls.
  • 2023 — Receives the Golden Saucer for Extraordinary Services to Hospitality and Mild Mischief.
  • Present — Developing a zero-gravity scone. Early tests show orbital jam.
“Octavia proved you can change the world with a kettle, a stopwatch, and the unwavering belief that joy should be precisely measured.”
— Prof. Lionel P. Whimsy, Chair of Practical Nonsense
